Your buyers and customers aren't just Googling anymore. They're asking ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI. The companies that show up in these AI answers are the ones making the shortlists. The ones that don't, are becoming invisible.

But how do these AI models *actually* find information? And why do they confidently recommend some businesses but not others?

We ran an analysis to find out.

## The Test: Snippet vs. Fact-Based Search

We asked three major AI models the same local query: *"Who are the best dentists in Dallas?"*

We found they answer in two fundamentally different ways:

### 1. The Snippet Summarizer (ChatGPT's Method)

This AI model works like a traditional web summarizer. It performs a live web search, reads the text snippets from the top results (like Yelp, Healthgrades, and local articles), and then synthesizes a summary of what it found.

* **Analogy:** A librarian who reads the first paragraph of 10 books and tells you what they're generally about.
* **Data Source:** Text snippets from search results.
* **Weakness:** It's summarizing *what people are saying* about a business, not the *facts* about the business. It often lacks verifiable details and, by its own admission, does not use structured data or Google Maps APIs.

### 2. The Entity-First Fact-Checker (Perplexity & Gemini's Method)

This is a modern, entity-based approach. These models query a structured database of *facts* about businesses, often called a "Knowledge Graph."

* **Analogy:** A database admin who runs a query against a verified 'business' table for a precise, factual report.
* **Data Source:** Verified Google Business Profiles, `schema.org` data, and JSON-LD (structured data) embedded in your website.
* **Strength:** The answer is built on verifiable, authoritative facts: official business names, verified addresses, phone numbers, and services offered.

## The So-What: Why Your Business is Invisible

Here is the critical insight: **If your business information is only written as text on your website, you are invisible to the fact-checkers.**

Models like Perplexity and Gemini's "Source of Truth" step *rely* on **machine-readable structured data (schema)** to find and verify you. Without it, you simply don't exist in their pool of verifiable answers.

When a user asks, "[Your Firm] does not appear to support [Your Service]," it's not because your website doesn't say it. It's because your website doesn't say it in the *language the AI understands.*
